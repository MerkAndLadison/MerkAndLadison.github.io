How We Met

His Story

The first time we met, I tried to make a joke (albeit a dumb and immature one), and was met with, "Don't you have anything better to say?" Needless to say, nothing happened after that for a while.

Fast forward a couple years to the first years of my post-grad life, and after several joint adventures... I realized something that many of you already know: Madison is pretty awesome. After deciding — during a soul-searching (or...work) trip to the Philippines — to reveal my feelings, I was not given a yes or no but instead a "Maybe. I like things about you..." And I waited.

Which has been a theme of our relationship. Patiently waiting and caring. Although she may have beaten me to saying the words "I love you," I reserve the right to claim that I hit that stage first emotionally. It's been wonderful and challenging learning new aspects of her that I never knew existed, and I'm excited to continue this process together!

I ask that you guys continue to be present in our lives as we go through this adventure, and I hope you can join us in our celebration!

Her Story

When Mark first told me he liked me, we were on a car ride to Charlottesville after a summer concert, with still an hour to go. (Later, my girlfriends all confirmed that this had been bad timing on Mark's part, but mysteriously, the boys don't seem to think so.) I questioned if he was "really being brave here," and he replied with something to the effect of: "My soul is trembling!!" I really had no idea.

Tentatively, timidly, my heart made the transition from [Friends] to [More Than] during my first/his second post-grad summer in Charlottesville. Mark impressively led the way for me as I learned the meaning of "adulting" during those first couple years after college, and it was his steady faithfulness, transparency of feeling, and amazing bravery that all convinced me that Mark was something special. He fell in like first, but I beat him to the "I love you" punch, on a friendcation to Chicago.

Ridiculous, cringe-worthy confession: Sometimes, I revel in all kinds of little details about Mark and about our relationship and think, "I am probably the luckiest girl in this entire place." I think the biggest place I thought that in was at a Passenger concert, so...a pretty impressive number of people to make such grand assumptions about. But that's how I feel — like the luckiest.

And in thinking about our future together, I can say the same — that we are so lucky to be able to share this momentous beginning with you all. Thank you, thank you, thank you for being with us, on our website, at our wedding, and in our lives as a married couple (soon!). We are so looking forward to it!
